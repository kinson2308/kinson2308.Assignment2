What is Travel Writing?

01

The Definition

A "hybrid genre" blending documentary observation with personal reflection. It captures the essence of a place through the lens of individual experience.

KEY INGREDIENTS

FACTUAL NARRATIVE PERSONAL VOICE
02

The Dual Goal

To Inform

Providing practical insights, cultural history, and real-world context.

To Inspire

Offering escapism for the "Armchair Traveler," transporting them to worlds they may never visit.

02

The Structure:
Double Journey

  • The Outer Journey The physical movement from A to B. Logistics, geography, and events.
  • The Inner Journey The psychological shift from Ignorance to Insight. The writer must be changed.
03

Technique 01

The Hook

In Media Res: Start in the middle of the action.

Avoid

"I woke up, packed my bag, and went to the airport."
(Too slow)

Try

"The boat engine died just as we hit the rapids."
(Immediate tension)

Rule: Grab the reader in the first paragraph.

04

Technique 02

Sensory Imagery

Concept: Show, Don't Tell

Case Study: Maximum City by Suketu Mehta (2004). Describing Mumbai by smell:

"A mixture of blue skin-cream, mildew, diesel fumes, and frying fish."

Tip: Don’t just show what the place looks like — show what it feels like.

05

Technique 03

Juxtaposition

Concept: Finding the Contrast

Reality is Complex: Places are rarely simple or perfect.

How to use: Place opposites side-by-side

  • Ancient vs. Modern
  • Silence vs. Noise
  • Wealth vs. Poverty
06

Technique 04

People Make the Place

Avoid the "Tourist Gaze." Landscapes are static, but people are dynamic.

Action: Let them speak. Quote them directly to give them a voice.

07

Technique 05

Cinematic Pacing

"Shifting the ‘camera’ of your writing to keep readers engaged."

We use what I call the Zoom Lens Approach

The Wide Shot
Setting the scene
The Close Up
Intimate details
